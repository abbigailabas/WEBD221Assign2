Hello, my name is Jenna Nemeth and I'm a senior at Fluvanna County High School as well as a member of the senior class' Blue Ridge Virtual Governor School program.

Ever since I was little, I've had a fascination with the mind and how it works and this year I partook in an internship with the school's psychologist, Mrs. Celine Carrier. Twice a week we would meet and talk about different aspects of psychology and the mind, specifically on developmental psychology. It was through her help that I decided and was able to complete my research paper.

Throughout all our talks, a common psychological debate was brought into conversation time and time again - the Nature vs. Nurture debate - and how it affected development. With this in mind, I decided for my research topic to decide which is more influential toward one's mental development, one's nature and genes, or one's nurture and experiences.

With Mrs. Carrier's help, we decided a good way to fulfill my community service was to mentor eighth grade students in order to help them set goals and gain an understanding of how to adapt to their life in high school in order to be successful. I met with two young girls once a week with Mrs. Carrier to have our "sessions." As for my legacy, I hope to establish a similar program with the Beta Club to continue once I graduate from high school.
